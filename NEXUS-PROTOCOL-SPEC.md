# Nexus Protocol — Game Spec

## Overview

1v1 abstract space-themed 4X game. Two factions on a hex grid race to construct a Nexus Gate at the center system. Neither faction can build it alone — cooperation and conflict are structurally inseparable.

**Format:** 1v1 · Hex grid · Fully visible · Simultaneous turns  
**Target length:** 20–30 minutes (~12–15 rounds)  
**4X:** Expand across colonies, Exploit resource income, Exterminate opposing fleets, race to the Nexus

---

## Map — 19 Hexes

```
         [ ]  [ ]  [ ]
       [ ]  [ ]  [ ]  [ ]
     [ ]  [ ]  [N]  [ ]  [ ]
       [ ]  [ ]  [ ]  [ ]
         [ ]  [ ]  [ ]
```

- **Nexus** `[N]` — center hex; win site only, no income, cannot be colonized
- **Home systems** — two opposing outer hexes, one per player; pre-colonized at game start
- **16 colony hexes** — every remaining hex, pre-assigned a color: 6 Red, 6 Blue, 4 Gold
- Gold colonies clustered near the center, creating a mid-map contest zone
- Map is symmetric; fully visible from turn 1

---

## Resources — Colors

Resources are abstract colors. Each player is assigned a faction color at game start.

| Color | Generated by | Required for |
|---|---|---|
| **Red** | Red home (+2/turn) · Red colonies (+1/turn) · trade routes | Fleet building · Nexus Gate |
| **Blue** | Blue home (+2/turn) · Blue colonies (+1/turn) · trade routes | Fleet building · Nexus Gate |
| **Gold** | Gold colonies only (+1/turn) · trade routes | Everything — fleet building and Nexus Gate |

**Key constraints:**
- Colonizing an opposing-color colony generates nothing — it only denies that income to your opponent
- Neither player can self-supply the opposing faction color; trade is the only path to it
- Gold cannot be generated natively by either player; it requires colonizing contested mid-map hexes
- Resources have no upper limit; unused resources carry over between turns

**Scalability:** Adding a third player adds a third faction color. The win condition scales to require all active colors. Gold may become that faction's native color or remain neutral — to be decided when extending to multiplayer.

---

## Units

- One unit type: **Fleets** (uniform strength: 1 each)
- Both players start with **2 fleets** on their home system
- Multiple fleets may stack on one hex
- New fleets built at home system only: **2 Red + 2 Blue + 2 Gold**; fleet appears the following turn

---

## Round Structure

Each round follows this sequence:

1. **Plan phase** — both players simultaneously and secretly commit orders
2. **Resolve phase:**
   1. Build Fleet cost deducted · Nexus Gate payment deducted
   2. Moves — all fleets move simultaneously; fleets may pass through each other without combat
   3. Combat — all contested hexes resolve simultaneously
   4. Colonization — Colonize orders execute; fails if combat occurred on that hex this turn
   5. Income — all income calculated and applied simultaneously
   6. Newly built fleets appear at home system
3. **Win check**

---

## Orders

Each fleet may be given one order per turn. A fleet with no order stays in place.

| Order | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Move** | Move to an adjacent hex; if the destination contains an opponent's fleet, combat resolves automatically after all moves complete |
| **Colonize** | Claim the current hex if unclaimed; income starts next turn. Invalid on a hex you already control or on the Nexus |

Multiple fleets stacked on the same hex may each be assigned different orders independently.

**Colonize fails after combat:** A Colonize order on a hex where combat occurred this turn is invalidated — the hex already reverted to unclaimed during combat, and the fleet must issue Colonize again next turn. This applies whether the fleet won the combat or submitted Colonize expecting to hold an uncontested hex that was then attacked. Both paths to capturing a hex require two turns minimum.

**Undefended opponent-controlled hex:** Moving into a hex that is opponent-controlled but has no fleet reverts the colony to unclaimed — the same outcome as winning combat. Your fleet is now on the hex but does not control it. You must issue Colonize the following turn to claim it. This means any undefended colony is always one turn away from being contested and a second turn away from changing hands.

Player-level orders (not fleet-specific):

| Order | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Build fleet** | At home system only; costs 2 Red + 2 Blue + 2 Gold; at most one fleet may be built per turn; fleet appears at end of Resolve and can receive orders the following turn |
| **Begin Nexus Gate** | Fleet must occupy the Nexus hex; commits resources toward construction |

## Trade Routes

Trade routes require no agreement. They emerge from fleet positioning.

**How a route forms:**
- A fleet **staying in place** (no move order) or **Colonizing** on your system automatically generates a trade route with every adjacent opponent-controlled system simultaneously
- Trade route income applies in the income phase of the same turn — there is no one-turn delay when a route first forms

**Income (per active route):**
- **Base:** each side receives +1 of the other system's color — just for the route being active
- **Fleet bonus:** if you have a fleet staying or colonizing at your endpoint, you receive +1 additional
- Fleet presence is binary — multiple fleets at the same endpoint do not stack the bonus

| Endpoint fleets | You receive | Opponent receives |
|---|---|---|
| Your fleet only (always the minimum) | +2 | +1 |
| Both players have a fleet at their end | +2 | +2 |

- One fleet can generate multiple simultaneous routes if adjacent to multiple opponent systems; income applies per route

**Opponent requirement:** Control only — the opponent does not need a fleet on their end to activate the route or receive the base +1. Posting a fleet at their endpoint earns them the +1 fleet bonus.

**Movement bonus:**
- A fleet that **starts its turn** on an active trade-route endpoint may move **2 hexes** instead of 1
- The bonus applies to both endpoint owners — both players move faster through the trade corridor
- A fleet that arrives at a trade-route endpoint mid-turn does not receive the bonus until the following turn

**Map geometry note:** Ring-1 hexes each sit one step from the Nexus and are adjacent to their two ring-1 neighbours. Two neighbouring ring-1 hexes and the Nexus form a triangle of mutual adjacency. Trade routes form on the flanks of each player's expansion, not across the center axis — a fleet at a ring-1 trade-route endpoint can reach the Nexus AND one hex past it in a single turn.

**Route ends when:**
- Your fleet moves away or is destroyed
- Either endpoint changes hands

**Routes cannot be unilaterally closed.** The only way to break a route is to accept the fleet moving away (losing your income too) or contest the endpoint via combat.

---

## Combat

When fleets from both players occupy the same hex after moves resolve:

- Higher fleet count wins
- Loser loses all fleets; winner loses fleets equal to the loser's count
- Equal counts: mutual destruction
- If the hex was a colony, it reverts to unclaimed after combat; neither player controls it until re-colonized

---

## Win Condition

**Build a Nexus Gate** at the center hex:

- Your fleet must occupy the Nexus and declare **Begin Nexus Gate**
- Total cost: **8 Red + 8 Blue + 8 Gold**, committed over **2 consecutive turns**
- **Turn N:** declare construction, commit half the resources; construction status is visible to both players
- **Turn N+1:** commit remaining resources; gate completes — you win
- Construction is cancelled and all committed resources are lost if: the constructing fleet is destroyed in combat; the fleet moves away voluntarily; or the player cannot commit the remaining resources on turn N+1
- The construction check happens after combat resolves — the fleet must survive combat on the Nexus for construction to proceed that turn
- If both players complete the Nexus Gate in the same turn: **draw**

**Backup rule:** If neither player has won by round 15, the player controlling more systems wins. Tied system count = draw.

## Resolve Phase Messages

One message is appended to the game log for each significant event during resolution. Messages are produced in resolve order.

### Moves

| Event | Message |
|---|---|
| Fleet moves (standard) | `[A]'s fleet moved from [Hex] to [Hex]` |
| Fleet moves via trade-route speed bonus | `[A]'s fleet advanced 2 hexes along the trade corridor from [Hex] to [Hex]` |
| Fleet enters undefended opponent-controlled hex | `[A]'s fleet entered [Hex] — colony reverts to unclaimed` |

### Combat (one block per contested hex)

| Event | Message |
|---|---|
| Combat initiated | `[A]'s [N] fleet(s) clashed with [B]'s [N] fleet(s) at [Hex]` |
| Attacker wins | `[A] takes [Hex] — [B] loses [N] fleet(s), [A] loses [N] fleet(s)` |
| Defender holds | `[B] holds [Hex] — [A] loses [N] fleet(s), [B] loses [N] fleet(s)` |
| Mutual destruction | `Mutual destruction at [Hex] — all fleets lost` |
| Colony reverts after combat | `[Hex] reverts to unclaimed` |
| Nexus Gate construction cancelled | `[A]'s Nexus Gate construction cancelled — committed resources lost` |

### Colonization

| Event | Message |
|---|---|
| Colony claimed | `[A] colonized [Hex] ([Color]) — income next turn` |
| Colonize failed (combat this turn) | `[A]'s Colonize at [Hex] failed — hex was contested this turn` |

### Income

| Event | Message |
|---|---|
| Trade route opened this turn | `A trade route opened between [A]'s [Hex] and [B]'s [Hex]` |
| Trade route closed this turn | `The trade route between [A]'s [Hex] and [B]'s [Hex] has closed` |
| Income received (one per player) | `[A] receives +[N] Red, +[N] Blue, +[N] Gold this turn` (home + colonies + routes collapsed into one line) |
| Fleet deployed (appears end of resolve) | `[A] deployed a new fleet at [Home]` |

### Win Check

| Event | Message |
|---|---|
| Gate construction begun (turn 1 of 2) | `[A] begins Nexus Gate construction — half resources committed (turn 1 of 2)` |
| Gate construction completes (turn 2 of 2) | `[A] commits final resources — Nexus Gate complete` |
| Victory by gate | `[A] wins — Nexus Gate constructed` |
| Draw by simultaneous gate | `Draw — both players completed the Nexus Gate simultaneously` |
| Backup win (round 15) | `[A] wins by colony count ([N] to [N])` |
| Backup draw (round 15) | `Draw — equal colony count at round 15` |

## UX — Game Screen

### Layout

Three-column layout with a persistent top bar:

- **Top bar:** game name · round counter · phase indicator · Submit Orders button (Planning phase only)
- **Left sidebar:** both players' resource totals — yours on top, opponent below; fully visible at all times (open information); pending orders list below resources during Planning phase
- **Center:** hex grid — primary interactive area; all orders assigned via grid interaction
- **Right sidebar:** game log and player chat interleaved chronologically; message input pinned at the bottom

### Hex Display

Each hex shows:
- **Background tint:** colony color (Red / Blue / Gold / neutral for Nexus and unassigned)
- **Border or fill:** owner indicator (your color / opponent color / unclaimed gray)
- **Fleet counts:** your fleets and opponent's fleets shown as separate counters
- **Trade route edges:** a colored line on the shared edge between two active trade route endpoints

### Order Assignment — Planning Phase

1. **Click a fleet** → selects it; valid destination hexes highlight (adjacent hexes; 2-step hexes also highlight if the trade route speed bonus applies to this fleet)
2. **Click a highlighted destination hex** → assigns Move; an arrow overlay appears on the grid
3. **Click the fleet's own hex** → assigns Colonize (if hex is unclaimed) or Begin Nexus Gate (if at the Nexus); no-op otherwise
4. **Click the selected fleet again** → clears the assigned order; fleet defaults to staying in place
5. **Click elsewhere** → deselects without changing orders

**Build Fleet:** click the home hex background (not a fleet icon) → a Build Fleet affordance appears showing the cost, greyed out if resources are insufficient or one has already been queued this turn; click again to confirm. This is the only order not tied to a specific fleet.

### Pending Orders List

During Planning phase, the bottom of the left sidebar shows all orders queued so far this turn:

- Each entry shows the fleet's current hex, the assigned order, and a remove button
- **Click a row** → selects that fleet on the grid (same as clicking it directly); valid targets highlight for reassignment
- **Click ×** → removes the order; fleet returns to no order
- Build Fleet appears as its own row when queued; × cancels it
- List is empty at the start of each Planning phase
- Entries are shown as arrow overlays on the grid simultaneously — the list and the grid are two views of the same state

### Multi-Select

- **Shift-click a fleet** → adds to selection
- **Shift-click a selected fleet** → removes from selection
- **Click a destination hex** → assigns Move to every selected fleet that can legally reach it; fleets that cannot are left with no order
- **Valid target highlighting:** union of all reachable hexes across selected fleets; hexes reachable by only some are dimmed to signal partial applicability
- **Click own hex (Colonize / Begin Nexus Gate):** applies to one fleet from the selection; others are unaffected

### Phase States

| Phase | Grid behaviour | Submitted orders |
|---|---|---|
| Planning | Fleets are clickable; orders assigned via grid | Arrow overlays on grid + pending orders list in sidebar |
| Waiting | Read-only | Your submitted orders remain visible as ghost arrows |
| Resolving | Read-only; map updates event-by-event in sync with log | Previous orders cleared |

### Resolve Display

Resolution plays out automatically in real time, visible to both players simultaneously. Neither player advances it manually — after all events complete, both enter the next Plan phase together.

Each resolve step animates on the hex grid and appends to the game log with a fixed delay between events:

| Step | Animation |
|---|---|
| Build cost deducted | Resource counters tick down (silent — confirmed at fleet appearance) |
| Moves | Fleet icons slide to destination hexes simultaneously |
| Combat | Contested hex flashes; fleet count numbers animate down |
| Colonization | Hex ownership color transitions |
| Income | Resource counters tick up |
| Fleet appears | New fleet icon fades in at home system |

**Timing:** Fixed delay — each log entry appears and its grid animation plays before the next event fires.

**Multiple simultaneous combats** are displayed sequentially in ring-inward order: ring-2 hexes first, ring-1 next, Nexus last. This gives a natural narrative feel of conflict sweeping toward the center.

## The Cooperation Loop

Neither player can win by pure aggression or pure isolation:

- **Red and Blue** come from your home territory and from trading with your opponent
- **Gold** comes only from contested mid-map colonies that both players want
- **Trade routes** open automatically from fleet positioning and benefit both players — closing one costs you income
- **The Nexus Gate** requires a visible 2-turn construction window, creating a diplomatic crisis at the end of every game: your opponent must choose between racing to contest the Nexus or using the time to build elsewhere

The central strategic question is always: cooperate now to accelerate, or disrupt now and accept the slowdown?

---

## Scalability Notes

The design is intentionally color-indexed rather than named-resource-indexed:

- Adding players = adding colors
- The win condition scales to require all active colors
- Trade route income scales to generate all active colors
- Fleet building costs scale to require all active colors
- No mechanical redesign is needed to extend to 3 or 4 players — only map size, colony distribution, and starting position adjustments
